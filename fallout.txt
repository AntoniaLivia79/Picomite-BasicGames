JUMPING FALLOUT
===============
A game by Neal Cavalier-Smith

SCENARIO
--------
A 9x9 grid is partially filled
with # obstacles. Nine digits
are waiting in the left column.
Your job is to shuffle rows so
the digits can fall through gaps
to the bottom and drop out.

Get all 9 digits to fall out
using as few moves as possible
to keep a high score.

THE GRID
--------
The grid has 9 columns and 9 rows.

  #        = An obstacle
  1 to 9   = Falling digits
  (space)  = Empty space

Digits fall DOWN through empty
spaces but are stopped by #.
When a digit falls off the
bottom edge it is counted out.

HOW TO PLAY
-----------
Each turn you are asked:

  LEVEL TO MOVE (1-8)
    Enter a row number to rotate.

  MOVES TO THE LEFT
    Enter how many steps to shift
    the row to the left. Cells
    that fall off the left edge
    wrap around to the right.

After each move, digits fall
through any new spaces created.

SCORING
-------
You start with 200 points.
Each move costs points:
  cost = row number + steps moved

Moving lower rows costs more.
Try to clear all digits with
as few moves as possible!

WIN CONDITION
-------------
Get all 9 digits to fall out
of the bottom of the grid.
Your final score is displayed.
Beat the current best to set
a new high score!

TIPS
----
Look for columns with gaps near
  the bottom already.
Move higher rows first - they
  cost fewer points.
Each column has at least one
  gap somewhere - find them!
One good shift can open a path
  for several digits at once.
